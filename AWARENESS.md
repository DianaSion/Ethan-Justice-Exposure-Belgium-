# Raising Awareness: ETHAN Case

This document provides guidance on spreading awareness about the systemic failures documented in this repository, both in Belgium and worldwide.

## Why Awareness Matters

### Making the Repository Immutable Through Society
This case becomes "immutable" not through technical blockchain systems, but through **widespread societal awareness**. When many people know about the case:
- Evidence becomes harder to suppress or ignore
- Institutional accountability increases
- Similar cases can be prevented
- Systemic reforms become possible
- Public pressure encourages legal action

### Public Interest Served
This case documents:
- Systematic failures in Belgium's legal system
- Violations of children's rights
- Institutional misconduct across multiple agencies
- Procedural defects that undermine justice
- Patterns that may affect other families

## Key Messages

### Core Message
"This repository documents systematic failures in Belgium's legal system that harmed a child despite overwhelming expert evidence. The case reveals procedural defects, suppression of evidence, disregard of medical assessments, and hidden institutionalization plans that violate children's rights."

### Supporting Messages

**1. Procedural Timing Defect**
"A juvenile court issued an out-of-home placement order BEFORE the police completed their investigation into the school's role‚Äîundermining the factual basis for the ruling."

**2. Expert Consensus Ignored**
"Every independent medical expert opposed removal and documented harm, yet institutional plans proceeded anyway. When do expert assessments matter?"

**3. Hidden Long-term Plans**
"Documents reveal a hidden plan for long-term institutionalization (to age 18-21) with subsidy requests‚Äîcontradicting the stated 'short diagnostics' purpose."

**4. Manufactured Non-cooperation**
"A reasonable request for a 1-on-1 safety meeting was refused, then weaponized as 'non-cooperation' to escalate to child welfare intervention."

**5. Outcome Matters**
"Although removal orders were issued, the child was never actually placed out of home‚Äîappeals and expert input prevented it. But the process itself caused documented psychological harm."
